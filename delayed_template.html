US Data Centers — Cancelled & Delayed

Sites that were originally supposed to come online in 2026–2028 but are now cancelled or slipping — mapped, with the GW and energy (TWh) that removes from the buildout.

Scope: only sites whose original online date (col BY, “Start of operations”) fell in 2026–2028. SemiAnalysis has no status flag, so status is derived: cancelled = the SemiAnalysis live date (col CC) is a far-future sentinel (≥2034) or blank while capacity is still planned; delayed = the live date slips >3 months past the original, colored on a yellow→red gradient by the length of the slip. Because most real cancellations were killed pre-construction by local opposition, SemiAnalysis under-counts them — so externally-reported cancellations/delays are overlaid as ringed markers (hover for the source), including a cluster in Indiana. Faint brown lines are the EIA gas-pipeline network (same basemap as the Geography page). Energy uses the model's own 8760h × utilization × PUE.

3. Does this contest the shortfall's “under construction” line?

Morgan Stanley's waterfall (page 1) is demand 67.6 − DC under-construction 14.9 − grid access 15.0 = 37.7 GW of data-center power capacity still needed, and it treats that under-construction capacity as reliably online in the window. So the test is: of the SemiAnalysis capacity slipping here, how much is committed (under construction) vs paper (Planned), and does the revised date still land by 2028 or slip past 2028?
